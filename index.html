About

There is a saying among mathematicians to the effect of “if you want to learn a subject, write a book about it.” It's very much in the spirit of that tongue-in-cheek wisdom that I've started this blog on machine learning. (Sadly, I had to downgrade book to blog in that paraphrase.) I will generally assume my reader has a high level of mathematical maturity, with mathematical concepts often taking center stage. That is not to say this blog is about theoretical machine learning or even the mathematics of theoretical machine learning. Rather, it's about organizing machine learning concepts around mathematical ones. Because many seemingly disjoint ideas in machine learning turn out to be in fact closely related from a mathematical perspective, this premise offers a kind of conceptual economy that often goes underappreciated.
